The basics

The rules of checkers are quite simple, there are pieces of two diffrent colors (some variation of red, black, or white) each color goes on opposite sides of the board on the dark spaces. Each color has one player controling that color and can only move one space diagonaly forwards (twords the opposite side of the board). A piece can also remove an opponent's peaces by hoping over them as long as the space directly behind said piece is unoccupied. First person to remove every one of the opponent's pieces wins.

Crowning

When a piece reaches the opposite side of the board known as the King's row, the player says "King me" and a second piece is placed on top of the first making it a King. A king can now move not only diagonaly forwards but also diagonaly backwards too. It is very important that you get your pieces to the king's row and remove your opponent's pieces as fast as posible due to how strong the king is.

Advanced

A piece can remove as many pieces as it wants in a single turn as long as the piece can be removed where the jumping piece lands. this can be helpful for not only geting a piece to the king's row faster, but also remove a lot of an opponent's pieces if the oppertunity rises. This also means that you have to be careful when playing your pieces less you want all of them to be gone in a instant. Some good advice is to "reinforce" your pieces by puting another one of your pieces behind another so that the opponent cant remove your pieces.
